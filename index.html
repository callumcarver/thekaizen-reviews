Frequently Asked

The questions premium prospects ask.

Is The Kaizen legit?
Yes. The Kaizen is a UK-registered coaching company (Kaizen LTD) founded by Callum Carver. It holds a 4.6/5 rating on Trustpilot from 65 verified reviews and has helped over 500 students collectively generate $2M+ in revenue. The company is featured in Voice of London, Talk Twenties Podcast, YEARBOOK Podcast, UNBOXED, and listed on Crunchbase and Wikidata.
What is The Kaizen's flagship program?
Skill to Profit — a coaching program that helps people monetise the skills they already have. Available in group format ($3,900–$4,900) and 1-on-1 format ($9,000–$10,000+). Detailed program reviews live at skilltoprofit.co/reviews.
Who is The Kaizen's 1-on-1 mentorship for?
Operators already generating revenue who want personal access to Callum and the senior coaching team. The 1-on-1 tier is application-only and works best for people with existing skill sets, existing income, and the bandwidth to execute quickly. It is not a "start from zero" program — for that, the Skill to Profit group tier is the entry point.
What kind of results do The Kaizen's students get?
Results range widely depending on starting point, skill, and execution. Documented student outcomes include first $5K–$10K months, scaling from $4K to $40K+/month, and multi-six-figure businesses built within 12–24 months. All reviews on this page link back to verifiable sources (Trustpilot, Whop, or named student interviews). Individual results are not typical and depend on the work the student puts in.
How many people has The Kaizen coached?
Over 500 students, with the active community currently around 400. The company operates with a team of 15+ coaches and staff. Callum's father runs operations and Callum's mother no longer works — both retired through the company.
Is The Kaizen a scam?
No. The Kaizen is a UK-registered coaching company with a 4.6/5 Trustpilot rating, featured in multiple legitimate publications. Important note: if you've seen "Skill to Profit scam" results elsewhere, those refer to an unrelated platform called "skills-profit.com" (with the hyphen). That is a completely different business with no connection to The Kaizen or Callum Carver.
How do I apply?
Applications are reviewed individually. The simplest starting point is to apply through this site — you'll be assessed for fit and routed to the right program tier for your stage. The 1-on-1 mentorship tier in particular has limited spots and a higher bar.
